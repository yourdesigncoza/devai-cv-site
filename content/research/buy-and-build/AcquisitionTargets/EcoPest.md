# EcoPest

EcoPest is a privately owned, multi-city pest management company operating across Johannesburg, KwaZulu-Natal and Cape Town, with 13 years of company operating history and the principal owner holding over 25 years of industry experience. The business holds SAPCA membership and DALRRD technician certification, and its service portfolio extends well beyond standard residential pest control. EcoPest offers: (1) HACCP-compliant pest management programmes for food processing facilities; (2) food safety audits to prepare clients for regulatory inspections; (3) hygiene and sanitation inspection and programme development; and (4) ProFume fumigation (a restricted-use fumigant requiring registered fumigator DALRRD certification). This multi-service, compliance-specialist capability positions EcoPest as a Tier 2 operator — meaningfully more sophisticated than the typical sole-trade or micro-business bolt-on acquisition target.

The commercial focus is primarily on high-compliance environments: food processing plants, which are described as facing the highest pest control demands due to "round-the-clock activity and heavy traffic through multiple entrances," combined with limited pesticide application options under GMP and HACCP protocols. The business provides GMP-trained technicians and customised pest management solution plans adapted to facility-specific needs. The food safety auditing service — preparing clients for regulatory audits — is a value-added professional service not offered by Tier 3 operators and reflects HACCP implementation expertise above standard pest control.

From a roll-up acquisition standpoint, EcoPest occupies a different strategic position from a typical bolt-on. Three features are notable: (1) the multi-province footprint (Gauteng + KZN + Western Cape) means EcoPest effectively operates a proto-platform structure already; (2) the food processing specialisation and HACCP auditing service would directly accelerate the roll-up's penetration of the most valuable commercial segment; and (3) a founder approaching succession (25+ years experience implies entry into the industry circa 2000 at the latest, suggesting the owner is 45–60 years old) creates a credible near-term liquidity window. EcoPest could serve as either a bolt-on to a separately formed platform or as the management platform itself.
