# Rentokil Initial South Africa

Rentokil Initial South Africa is the local subsidiary of [[Rentokil Initial plc]], the world's largest pest control company by revenue. Operating in South Africa since 1967, when Rentokil Laboratories (Pty) Ltd was first registered, the business now operates under the OneRISA (Rentokil Initial South Africa) brand umbrella. The South African operation runs 12 branches stretching from Polokwane in the north to Cape Town in the south, employing approximately 290 people according to third-party estimates (evidence_strength: alleged — Apollo.io, unverified).

Three service brands sit under the OneRISA structure: Rentokil (commercial and domestic pest control), Initial (washroom and workplace hygiene), and Ambius (interior landscaping). All pest control technicians are required to hold SAPCA qualification, and the business claims full compliance with the Occupational Health and Safety Act 85 of 1993. The company has invested in remote monitoring and digital service management tools, positioning itself at the technology frontier of the South African market.

[[Rentokil Initial plc]]'s parent group reported revenue of £5.37bn in 2023 (up 44.7% YoY, largely driven by the December 2021 acquisition of Terminix for $7.6bn). Pest control accounts for approximately 64% of group ongoing revenue globally. South Africa is described in parent-company materials as a country where Rentokil holds a "leading position", alongside the UK, continental Europe, Asia and Pacific.

In 2015, Rentokil Initial (Pty) Ltd formed the [[Rentokil Initial Dikapi]] joint venture with Dikapi Cleaning Services (Pty) Ltd, a Phokeng-based cleaning company, achieving a Level 2 BBBEE rating and 55.75% black ownership. This JV structure is strategically important for accessing government and large-corporate procurement that mandates BBBEE-compliant suppliers. All branches hold [[South African Pest Control Association]] membership certificates, several of which are published on the company's South African website.

[[Rentokil Initial South Africa]] is simultaneously the most formidable potential incumbent competitor and a potential exit acquirer for any buy-and-build roll-up in the South African pest control market. As the global consolidator in pest control, Rentokil has a documented acquisition appetite (50+ US acquisitions, plus deals in Turkey, Brazil, UAE); however, its South African footprint (12 branches) appears modest relative to its global scale, suggesting either organic-only growth or an unlisted bolt-on history that is not publicly disclosed.
