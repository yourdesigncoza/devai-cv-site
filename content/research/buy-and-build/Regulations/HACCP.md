# HACCP

HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points) is an internationally recognised food safety management framework adopted in South Africa through Regulation R908 of 2003 (Department of Health) and referenced in SANS 10330:2007. In South Africa, businesses in food production, food handling, retail and hospitality are required to operate effective pest control programmes as a prerequisite or Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) element of HACCP compliance. The applicable standard for pesticide application in food-handling environments is SANS 10133, which sets out specific requirements for approved products, application methods and documentation.

HACCP compliance is among the most powerful recurring revenue drivers in the South African pest control market. Food manufacturers, food retailers and hospitality businesses cannot certify or maintain HACCP compliance without documented, continuous pest control programmes — creating contractually sticky, non-price-sensitive demand. Pest control contracts in food-handling facilities typically involve monthly (or more frequent) inspection cycles, device monitoring, fogging and drain treatments. [[Servest]] cites a reference contract with a food manufacturing facility covering 185 units on monthly inspection; [[Rentokil Initial South Africa]] similarly positions HACCP compliance as a key commercial value proposition.

The [[Gauteng Department of Health]] (via R908/2003) and [[DALRRD]] (via Act 36 of 1947 and DAFF regulations) jointly shape the pest control compliance requirements for food businesses. Non-compliance with HACCP pest control requirements risks loss of food safety certification, rejection of export products, and regulatory action. For a roll-up investor, HACCP-linked contracts represent the most attractive segment of the market: high stickiness, non-discretionary spend, recurring monthly billing, and audit documentation that creates switching costs.
