# South African Pest Control Market

The South African pest control market is estimated at approximately USD 198.42 million (~R3.7bn at 2025 exchange rates) in 2025, growing at a CAGR of 7.1% according to Cognitive Market Research — one of the faster growth rates globally, exceeding Africa's average (6.2%) and the global average (~5.7%). Note that these figures are from third-party market research firms and should be treated as indicative estimates (evidence_strength: alleged) pending primary verification from SAPCA, DALRRD or audited company financials.

The market is characterised by three tiers. Tier 1 consists of large integrated operators with national footprints: [[Rentokil Initial South Africa]], [[Bidvest Steiner]] (hygiene-led), and [[Servest]] (FM-led). These incumbents serve large commercial, government and food-sector clients under bundled or integrated service contracts. Tier 2 consists of regional operators with 2–10 branches serving mid-market commercial clients. Tier 3 — and the primary acquisition target universe for a roll-up — consists of small owner-operated PCOs with 1–3 technicians, often run by the founder/registered PCO, serving local domestic and small-commercial markets. Tier 3 is estimated to represent the majority of registered PCOs in South Africa.

Globally, the pest control industry has demonstrated compelling roll-up economics: 70–85% recurring revenue, 25–35% EBITDA margins for well-run operators (The Deal Sheet, 2026; evidence_strength: alleged for SA specifically), and acquisition multiples of 3–6x EBITDA for small operators rising to 8–12x at exit to a strategic acquirer. The global roll-up playbook (Rollins, Rentokil, Anticimex) has been executed in the US, UK, Europe and Australia, but has not yet been applied at scale in South Africa — suggesting a first-mover window for a local consolidator.

Key structural tailwinds for the SA market include: rising urbanisation and food safety compliance awareness; tightening of Act 36 of 1947 regulations (2023 amendment); growing hospitality and food manufacturing sectors; BBBEE procurement requirements that favour structured, certified operators; and the increasing burden of CPD compliance on sole-trader PCOs creating owner-exit pressure.
